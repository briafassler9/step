Soccer

I started playing soccer when I was only 5 years old. My parents were forcing me to try every sport possible, but soccer was what really stuck. I started playing for recreational teams and soon fell in love with the sport. Around the end of elementary and the beginning of middle school, I joined a club team. During the offseason, I would play for an indoor league in the winter, and a recreational team in the spring. After 8th grade, I was too old for the club team and instead decided to tryout for my high school's soccer team. I made varsity and was a starter for all 4 years, along with being captain for 3. In college, I decided to focus more on my studies instead of becoming a student athlete. Soccer will always have a special place in my heart.

Lacrosse

In my early teenage years my soccer team introcuded me to lacrosse, so i decided to give it a chance. I joined a recreational team where I knew some of my soccer teammates, but didn't realize how complicated the sport was so I ended up playing goalie. After one season, I quit and didn't return until sophomore year of high school. I made the varsity team and had to relearn everything about lacrosse. I was a starter for 2 years until I was too busy my senior year to play both soccer and lacrosse. Lacrosse definitely wasn't my favorite sport, but it did keep me in shape for the time being.

Track and Field

In 8th grade, I wanted to particpate in a sport at my middle school. All of my friends were joining track and I knew the coach, so I decided to try out. I trained for the 4x100m, 400m, and 4x400m races. Our school was #1 in the county, and we proudly wore that on our sleeves. In 9th grade, I tried out for the outdoor track and field team and made varsity. In high school I was a 4x800m runner, and had a good time at all of the track meets. The following winter, I tried out for indoor track and field, made varsity, and prepared for the same events. After the first meet, I decided track was no longer for me and left the team. That was the end of my track and field journey!
